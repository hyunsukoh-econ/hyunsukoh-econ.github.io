Job Market Paper

The Causal Effects of Winter Weather on Telework Hours: Evidence from CPS Panel Data

Draft available upon request

Abstract

This paper estimates the causal effects of winter weather conditions on telework hours using Current Population Survey panel data from October 2022 to March 2025. Exploiting within individual variation in weather exposure across CPS interview months, I employ a two way fixed effects identification strategy combined with continuous treatment difference in differences methods. I find that adverse winter weather — measured by temperature, wind speed, and snow depth — significantly increases telework hours, with heterogeneous effects across occupational telework feasibility quartiles, geographic divisions, education levels, and age groups. The results distinguish substitution effects from output effects on total working hours, contributing to our understanding of how environmental conditions shape the evolving geography of work.
